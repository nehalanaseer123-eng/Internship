Alappuzha is famous for its scenic backwaters and houseboat cruises. alappuzha Known as the "Venice of the East," it is surrounded by canals, lagoons, and lakes. The annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race is a major attraction for tourists. Alappuzha Beach offers stunning sunsets and a historic pier. The region is lush with coconut palms, paddy fields, and serene landscapes. Alappuzha is an important center for the coir industry and handicrafts. Tourists visit Alappuzha to experience the tranquil beauty of Kerala’s backwater network. Location: Lies along the Arabian Sea coast, in central Kerala. Nickname: Known as the “Venice of the East” because of its beautiful network of canals, rivers, and lagoons. Backwaters: Famous for houseboat cruises on the Vembanad Lake and backwater tourism. Tourism: Popular attractions include Alappuzha Beach, Marari Beach, Pathiramanal Island, and Ambalappuzha Sree Krishna Temple. Events: Renowned for the Nehru Trophy Boat Race held annually on Punnamada Lake. Economy: Based on coir industry, tourism, fishing, and agriculture (paddy fields of Kuttanad). Kuttanad: Known as the “Rice Bowl of Kerala”, one of the few places in the world where farming is done below sea level. Culture: Strong traditions in Kathakali, temple festivals, and coir handicrafts. Connectivity: Well connected by road, rail, and waterways; nearest airport is Cochin International Airport. Identity: A perfect blend of backwater beauty, beaches, temples, and cultural heritage.
