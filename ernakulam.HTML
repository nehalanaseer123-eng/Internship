ERNAKULAM

Ernakulam – the heart of Kochi City, full of life and light! A hub of transport – from ferries to metros! Near the beautiful backwaters and islands of Kerala! Culture blend: Temples, Churches, and Mosques stand side by side. Shop at Broadway and eat at local thattukadas! Home to top colleges, IT parks, and offices. Enjoy art at Durbar Hall Art Gallery and drama at JT Pac! Relax at Subhash Park by the lake with a peaceful vibe. Explore the busy MG Road and its colorful street life! Taste the flavors of Kerala – spicy fish curry & coconut in every bite! Location: Situated in central Kerala, along the Arabian Sea coast. Commercial Hub: Known as the “Commercial Capital of Kerala”, home to Kochi (Cochin), one of India’s busiest port cities. Tourism: Famous for Fort Kochi, Mattancherry Palace, Chinese Fishing Nets, Marine Drive, Bolgatty Palace, and Cherai Beach. History: Kochi was a major spice trading center, influenced by the Portuguese, Dutch, and British; still has a cosmopolitan heritage. Culture: Blend of Kerala traditions with colonial influences; famous for Kathakali, Kalaripayattu, and cultural festivals like the Kochi Biennale (international art exhibition). Economy: Major center for trade, IT, shipping, and industry; houses Cochin Shipyard, Cochin Port Trust, Infopark, and Cochin Special Economic Zone. Connectivity: Excellent transport links – Cochin International Airport, metro rail, railways, and national highways. Education & Research: Home to institutions like CUSAT (Cochin University of Science & Technology), Rajagiri institutions, and medical colleges. Specialty: Known for its backwaters, spices, seafood, and as a cosmopolitan city with diverse communities (Malayali, Jewish, Anglo-Indian, Arab, and Portuguese influences). Identity: Often referred to as the “Queen of the Arabian Sea” for its maritime importance.
