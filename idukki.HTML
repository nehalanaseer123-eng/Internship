IDUKKI

Idukki – Kerala’s hill paradise! Famous for lush green hills & deep valleys. Home to the Idukki Arch Dam – the biggest of its kind in Asia! Known for wildlife sanctuaries & elephant sightings. Misty mornings, cool breeze – perfect for nature lovers! Don’t miss tea plantations & spice gardens. Trek, explore, relax – Idukki has it all! Location: Situated in the Western Ghats of Kerala, known for its hills, forests, and wildlife. Geography: One of the most forested districts in Kerala, with more than half of its area covered in dense forests. Tourism: Famous for attractions like Munnar (hill station), Thekkady (Periyar Tiger Reserve), and Idukki Arch Dam (Asia’s first arch dam). Climate: Pleasant and cool, especially in hill stations like Munnar. Agriculture: Known for spices (cardamom, pepper), tea, and coffee plantations. Wildlife: Home to sanctuaries such as Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary and Eravikulam National Park (where the endangered Nilgiri Tahr is found). Hydropower: Major source of Kerala’s electricity, with several dams and hydroelectric projects. Culture: Inhabited by a mix of Malayalis and tribal communities, with rich traditions and folklore. Connectivity: Rugged terrain makes accessibility challenging; no railway line, but connected by roadways. Specialty: Known as the "Spice Garden of Kerala" due to its vast spice plantations.
