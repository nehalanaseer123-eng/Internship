KOZHIKODE

Kozhikode is a beautiful coastal city in Kerala, India. Known for its serene backwaters and lush greenery . The city is famous for its delicious Malabar biryani . Rich in history, it was a major trading port for spices and silk . Kozhikode Beach is perfect for sunsets and evening strolls . Home to many scholars and known as the “City of Spices” . Easily accessible by air, road , and train . Diverse culture with a mix of Muslim, Hindu, and Christian traditions . Great spot for photography lovers with scenic spots . Don’t miss the famous Kozhikode halwa – sweet and sticky! Location: Situated on the Malabar Coast of Kerala, facing the Arabian Sea. Historical Importance: Known as the place where Vasco da Gama first landed in 1498, opening the sea route between Europe and India. Cultural Significance: Once the capital of the Zamorin rulers, an important trade center for spices, especially black pepper. Tourism: Popular attractions include Kappad Beach, Beypore Port, Mananchira Square, and SM Street (Sweet Meat Street) for shopping. Economy: A hub for trade, commerce, and education in northern Kerala; famous for handlooms and the historic Beypore shipbuilding industry (Uru boats). Cuisine: Renowned for Malabar biryani, halwa, and unique seafood dishes. Education: Home to prestigious institutions like IIM Kozhikode, Calicut University, and NIT Calicut nearby. Connectivity: Well connected by railway, highway, and Calicut International Airport (Karipur). Culture & Arts: Known for traditional art forms like Oppana, Theyyam, and Thirayattam; also a hub for Malayalam literature and publishing. Identity: Often called the “City of Spices” because of its historic role in the spice trade.
